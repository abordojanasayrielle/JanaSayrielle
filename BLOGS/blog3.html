This Is the Future I’m Coding for Myself, One Bug at a Time

BY: JANA SAYRIELLE G. ABORDO | JANUARY 28, 2026

Introduction | Body | Conclusion

“Sure ka? IT ka?” I’ve heard that question more times than I can count—usually followed by a look that says hindi ka para dito. Being a woman in IT means getting doubted before I even get the chance to prove myself. But instead of letting that stop me, it pushed me to think harder about the future I want in this field—one where I don’t just survive, but dominate a space that keeps underestimating me. This blog is about the career I’m building in tech and the goals I’m coding for myself—one bug, one challenge, and one win at a time. You’ll know who I am soon.

My Opening Tag

The Career I’m Building

I know exactly the career I want to build. On the surface, gusto ko lang matapos school with flying colors, maybe even aim for a doctorate someday. I want a flexible work setup sa application development or web designing—kung saan pwede akong gumawa ng projects na may impact, pero hindi rin ako nakukulong sa office. Recently, I just finished a project for my HCI class, redesigning a website for a makeup brand called Cosmetics. Using Wix, ginawa ko ang homepage nila with promotional pictures instead of the original flower garden design, kasi honestly, it didn’t match the brand vibe at all. I even thought, “This is actually sellable! Pwede natin ipropose sa brand!” Moments like that make me feel like I’m already stepping into the career I want—even as a student.

I also want to explore part-time jobs kasi I love learning new things. Dream ko rin mag-teach sa top university tulad ng DLSU, and someday, gusto kong makatrabaho sa ibang bansa para ma-expand yung knowledge and experience ko. Alam kong hindi madali, pero I like challenges. Yung sense na kahit maliliit na projects ngayon, may role sila sa bigger picture ng future ko, sobra saya lang.

My Attribute

The Skills I’m Grinding

Hindi lang coding ang focus ko. Right now, I’m grinding on Python, HTML/CSS, Java, SQL, C#, at gusto ko pang matutunan na ibang languages as I go. Sa Integrative Programming 1, sobrang interesting mag-code to design pages. Nasanay na kasi ako sa Canva na drag-drag lang, pero dito kailangan talaga ng utak. Super time-consuming, pero yung knowledge at yung sense of achievement kapag finally gets mo paano gumagana, sobra rewarding.

Hindi rin lang tech skills—part din ng growth ko ang teamwork, problem-solving, at communication skills. Group projects, coding collaborations, at presentations are shaping the way I’ll handle real-world IT challenges. Every bug I fix, every assignment I finish, is one step closer sa pagiging confident at competent IT professional na gusto kong maging.

Gusto ko rin subukan ang database work kasi I really enjoyed our Database class using SQL at MS Access, at ma-explore din ang cybersecurity, especially sa government. It’s thrilling, and yk, the job pays well kasi sobrang crucial ng work. I want my presence to be felt in every project I take—every move deliberate, every achievement stacking up, building a legacy I can be proud of.

My Value

Dream Projects & Goals

Gusto kong gumawa ng projects na may impact. Like projects na nagpapakita na women belong at kaya rin mag-lead sa IT. Every challenge ngayon, whether it’s coding, redesigning, or learning a new language, is shaping the professional I aim to be. I want my work to matter, hindi lang sa resume, kundi pati sa mga taong makaka-benefit nito.

Some of my dream projects include web applications, user-friendly apps, databases, at cybersecurity solutions. I want people to remember my work, at makita na kahit babae ako sa male-dominated field, kaya kong i-own yung space. Every small project ngayon is building toward the big impact I want to make someday.

My Closing Tag

Reflection

Hindi ko pa alam exact step-by-step roadmap kung paano ko maa-achieve lahat ng goals ko, pero one thing’s for sure: every project I take, every line of code I write, is me actively coding the future I want. Being a woman in IT hasn’t been easy—madaming doubts, stereotypes—but bawat challenge, I turn it into fuel para mas maging better at confident ako.

Dream big, work harder, and stay curious. I’m building my skills, exploring opportunities, and setting the foundation for a career na hindi lang successful, kundi meaningful. One bug, one challenge, one win at a time—I’m coding the future I deserve, and I know it’s gonna be worth it.
